Tutor with Competitors

Cost: Apparently, the cost of a tutor is the greatest concern that stops many parents, either middle or low income, from affording help for their children to improve their grades. Most centers charge anywhere from $50 to $120 per hour. With an average of three sessions per week, it costs $600 to $1440 per month, which is out of reach for many families. *The provided rate is a sample for a Math tutor.
Time Spent: According to research from Brown University tutoring is most likely to be effective when delivered in high doses through tutoring programs with three or more sessions per week. Unfortunately, due to budget constraints, many students have limited hours or none at all. Parents aim to maximize their child's learning time while also limiting the cost factors.
Effort: In-person tutoring can be highly beneficial for students, but it also comes with some challenges, particularly in terms of driving time. Driving to and from tutoring sessions can consume a significant amount of time, especially for families with busy schedules. This can be challenging for both students and parents, as it may conflict with other commitments and activities.
Flexibility: Scheduling with the right expert for a student's situation at the tutor center can sometimes present challenges due to various factors. Tutor centers often have limited availability, especially during peak times such as exam periods or after-school hours. Some centers may have rigid operating hours that don't align with a student's schedule or availability. Fixed session durations may not align with a student's learning pace, as some students may require longer or shorter sessions.
